About the job

Job summary

UKHSA is looking for a knowledgeable, hard-working and enthusiastic Information Governance and Privacy Support Officer. Working in the Privacy team in the Information Management and Privacy (IMP) division, you will help provide an information governance and privacy support and assurance service to ensure that UKHSA fully complies with data protection law while maximising the public health benefit of the data and information it collects and uses to fulfil its remit as the national expert agency for health protection. 

You will have detailed knowledge of data protection and privacy legislation, including the Data Protection Act and UK General Data Protection Regulation, and be aware of the guidance issued by the Information Commissioner’s Office. A good understanding of the different types of data and information is vital, as is an awareness of the principles of data protection and information risk management.

You will have experience of working in an information governance and privacy service, preferably in the health and care sector. Experience of working in a team and an understanding of how to build positive and effective working relationships with colleagues are important.

You will be a self-starter who is able to work well under pressure, support several projects at the same time, and work effectively for periods without direct supervision. You will have strong writing skills and be confident when presenting your work and providing advice and support to others.

Job description

The main duties and responsibilities of the Information Governance and Privacy Support Officer are to: 

  • Support the production and assurance of data protection impact assessments and privacy notices
  • Provide administrative support to the Privacy team, such as maintaining the corporate register of processing activities, managing the Privacy team mailbox, and supporting the response to information governance and privacy incidents

Detailed job description and responsibilities:

  • Information governance and privacy support: provide support to key staff, particularly information asset owners, with the identification and management of information governance and privacy risks and issues
  • Data protection impact assessment and privacy notice support and assurance: support the production and maintenance of DPIAs, privacy notices and other assurance and information risk management documents
  • Maintaining corporate registers: support the update and maintenance of the corporate data protection impact assessment, privacy notice and related registers
  • Building a strong data protection culture: support the triage and timely response to requests for information governance and privacy advice, and support the reporting and investigation of information governance and privacy incidents and near-misses 
  • Project management: support a range of information governance and privacy projects and initiatives to help strengthen the management of data and information risks and issues across UKHSA
  • Cross-team working: work collaboratively across other teams in the IMP division – including data acquisition and sharing, data governance, and information and records management – on joint projects and initiatives
  • Training provision: support the provision of information governance and privacy training to individuals and teams
  • Representing the Privacy team: represent the Privacy team as required at internal information governance and privacy meetings, groups and forums
  • Personal development: identify, discuss and action professional performance and training and development needs through the UKHSA appraisal and individual development plan processes; attend internal and external training events as required; and participate in UKHSA mandatory training as required

Person specification

Essential criteria

  • Educated to degree level in a relevant subject, such as public health, law or subjects with a strong data science/analysis focus, or some experience working in an information governance or data protection role
  • Within- and cross-team working skills
  • Good interpersonal and communication skills
  • Good writing and presentation skills
  •  Ability to work to deadlines and under pressure
  • Ability to prioritise between competing pressures and demands
  • Ability to work for periods without direct supervision
  • An understanding of and commitment to equality of opportunity and good working relationships

Desirable criteria:

  • Data security and protection qualification, such as BCS Practitioner Certificate in Data Protection, CIPP/E, etc.
  • Project management qualification such as Agile Project Management Foundation
  • Knowledge of data protection law
  • Knowledge of the health and care system
  •  Experience of working in an information governance and privacy service
  • Project management and delivery skills

Things you need to know

Selection process details

This vacancy will be assessed using a competency-based framework which will assess your qualifications, knowledge and experience and / or skills and abilities outlined in the essential criteria.  

Stage 1: Application & Sift  

You will be required to complete an application form. You will be assessed on the listed 7 essential criteria, and this will be in the form of a:  

  • Application form (‘Employer/ Activity history’ section on the application)
  • up to 1,500 word supporting statement.  

This should outline how your skills, experience, and knowledge, provide evidence of your suitability for the role, with reference to the essential criteria.  

The Application form and supporting statement will be marked together. 

Please note you will not be able to upload your CV. You must complete the application form in as much detail as possible. Please do not email us your CV.  

Longlisting:

In the event of a large number of applications we will longlist into 3 piles of: 

  1. Meets all essential criteria 
  2. Meets some essential criteria  
  3. Meets no essential criteria  

The following will be taken through to the next stage:

  1. Meets all essential criteria 

Shortlisting:

In the event of a large number of applications we will shortlist on the following: 

  • Educated to degree level in a relevant subject, such as public health, law or subjects with a strong data science/analysis focus, or some experience working in an information governance or data protection role 

Desirable criteria may be used in the event of a large number of applications / large amount of successful candidates.

If you are successful at this stage, you will progress to interview & assessment.  

Please do not exceed 1,500words.  We will not consider any words over and above this number. 

Feedback will not be provided at this stage.

Stage 2: Interview (competency based) 

You will be invited to a (single) remote interview.

Knowledge, experience, skills & abilities will be tested at interview.

You will be asked to prepare a 5 minute verbal presentation which will assess your knowledge of the role, communication, and presentation skills. Further details will be provided at interview stage.
